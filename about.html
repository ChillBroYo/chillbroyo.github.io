Who I Am

A neighbor who listens, a nurse who advocates, and a leader who brings community voices into rooms where decisions are made.

From Immigrant Roots

I arrived in San Diego at eight months old, born in Sudan to Eritrean refugee parents who fled conflict to build a new life here. Education was my family’s path to opportunity and stability.

Growing up, my siblings and I were bused out of underperforming District 4 schools. In 1999, we settled in Emerald Hills — a Black, working-class neighborhood rich in pride, resilience, and community.

A Nurse, a Single Mom, an Advocate

I became a mother as a teenager and raised my son while pursuing my nursing degree at SDSU. For over a decade, I worked in the NICU caring for our most vulnerable lives.

I later earned my Master’s Degree in Nursing and founded a home-care agency for seniors — bringing dignity, compassion, and medical expertise to people aging at home.

Advocating for Justice & Equity

I organized against discriminatory zoning policies like Footnote 7, helping lead a grassroots effort that forced the city to repeal it in 2025.

When developers proposed hundreds of ADUs on single-family lots without infrastructure support, our community organized again — and won.
