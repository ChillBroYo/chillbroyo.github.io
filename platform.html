Our Campaign Priorities

I am committed to advocating for and helping build a San Diego where all communities can thrive, be housed, and live quality lives. My identities and lived experiences have enabled me to see our system and societies from the margins, and I hope to use that knowledge alongside communities and other responsive leaders to build a better District 4 and San Diego for all of us. I will bring integrity, accountability, equity and inclusion back to City Hall striving to reduce social and economic disparity and empower our community and future generations.

People First

  • Underpinning the needs and priorities of District 4 and its residents is my strong belief in community involvement. I am committed to transparency and collaboration with the community. I will provide forums and regular opportunities to hear from and learn from the residents and businesses of District 4.
  • Every decision made with residents, not for them
  • Restoring trust by bringing residents into the decision-making process, listening, and holding the city accountable
  • Prioritizing transparency, equitable investment, and meaningful community engagement so residents have a voice in shaping their future

Community Development and Housing

  • For decades, District 4 has been used as a dumping ground for developments that wealthier communities would never accept, concentrating poverty while denying us investment, infrastructure, and opportunity
  • Stable, safe, decent housing is the foundation of public health. When families are displaced by thoughtless redevelopment, children lose their schools, elders lose their support networks, and stress-related illnesses rise.
  • Housing justice and responsible redevelopment means healthier, more stable lives. I will fight for and demand:
  • Strengthened tenant protections, eviction defense, and pathways to homeownership for residents.
  • Upholding and funding the Community Plans in District 4, ensuring residents, not developers, shape our future
  • Pushing for implementation of Affirmatively Furthering Fair Housing citywide so that lower income families have the option to live in areas that have resources and opportunities that will enhance their quality of life
  • Fair-share development, creating communities for people of all socio-economic levels, across all council districts, not just District 4.
  • An end to policies that deepen segregation by overloading District 4 with high-density subsidized units while wealthier districts are protected.

Infrastructure That Serves the People

  • Infrastructure is more than roads, sidewalks, streetlights, and parks, it's a visual representation of fairness, dignity and opportunity. We have seen that when infrastructure fails or is non-existent, the price is paid by our physical and mental health. Investing in infrastructure is investing in people, it’s how we honor the resilience of our community and right the decades of historic neglect. Investing in infrastructure improves people’s health and quality of life. Through equity-driven budgeting and intentional rebuilding, we can safely connect people to jobs, students to schools, and neighbors to one another.
  • Equity-Driven Capital Budgeting Push for a dedicated Infrastructure Fund for low and moderate resource areas, based on the California Tax Credit Allocation Committee/Housing and Community Development Opportunity Area Maps, within the city budget, so we don’t have to beg for basic repairs. Require and create accessible avenues for community input before any public works project is approved; residents know where the gaps are better than any city map. Partner with communities to coordinate infrastructure upgrades that meet multiple needs like mobility, public health, and public safety.
  • Rebuild the Basics: Sidewalks, Lighting, Drainage, and Parks Invest in sidewalks, street lighting, and stormwater drainage in every District 4 neighborhood, not just in response to Get it Done complaints, but as part of a proactive, justice-based capital improvement plan.
  • Eliminate infrastructure deserts in areas where missing or broken infrastructure puts children, elders, and people with disabilities at risk.
  • Create safer intersections and traffic calming in residential areas to prevent accidents and protect children.
  • Ensure an equitable share of climate resilience funding and resources.
  • Upgrade storm drains to prevent flooding during storms (especially in low-lying areas).
  • Implement green infrastructure like rain gardens, bioswales, and drought-resilient landscaping.
  • Parks as Public Health: Revitalize existing parks and acquire new ones where access is limited. Invest in upgrades to playgrounds, restrooms, walking trails, and shade structures.
